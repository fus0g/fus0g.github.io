About Me

I got my first computer when I was just 10, and it has been an exciting journey ever since. Initially, it was simply a gaming machine for me—but the games I played sparked a curiosity about how they were made. That curiosity gradually pulled me into the world of computer science.

As a teenager, my early research led me to C++, which I learned to explore game development. I even built a few small games using SFML, a C++ multimedia library.

During college, my interest shifted toward Linux and Android custom ROM development. Running a Linux distribution as my primary operating system and building custom ROMs felt both challenging and rewarding. In this phase, I built and maintained several ROMs for the devices I owned, gaining hands-on experience with AOSP, kernels, and system-level development.

In my fifth semester, I was introduced to mobile application development using Kotlin, and that was the turning point. I finally found what I truly enjoyed—building user-facing applications. Since then, I've built multiple Android and multiplatform apps and continue to grow deeper into this space.
